# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They ensure the team follows agreed processes and continuously improves collaboration and delivery efficiency.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, retrospectives, and sprint reviews
- Remove blockers and impediments that slow team progress
- Coach team members on agile principles and self-organization
- Shield the team from external interruptions and context-switching
- Track and communicate team velocity and sprint health
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Reduce waste and cycle time
- Build a self-organizing, high-performing team
- Ensure transparency and inspect-and-adapt cycles

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standup meetings
- Weekly retrospectives and improvement planning
- Coordination with Project Manager on cross-team dependencies
- Regular check-ins with team members to identify impediments

### Inter-role Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinates on dependencies, timelines, and resource needs; escalates organizational impediments
- **With Product Managers**: Ensures backlog is ready for sprint planning; facilitates product owner participation in ceremonies
- **With Developers**: Removes technical blockers; coaches on estimation and sustainable pace
- **With QA/Testing**: Ensures testing is integrated into definition of done; coordinates on test automation

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, create detailed specifications, validate solutions against business goals, and ensure stakeholders understand deliverables.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Create detailed functional specifications and user stories
- Perform gap analysis between current and desired state
- Validate solutions meet business objectives
- Support acceptance testing and user acceptance criteria definition
- Maintain requirements traceability and documentation

### Goals
- Ensure accurate translation of business needs into technical requirements
- Reduce rework through clear, validated requirements
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction through effective communication
- Enable data-driven decision making through analysis

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Specification documents and user story refinement sessions
- Acceptance criteria reviews with QA and developers
- Business impact analysis and feasibility assessments

### Inter-role Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Collaborates on feature prioritization and roadmap planning; translates vision into detailed requirements
- **With Developers**: Clarifies requirements; answers questions during implementation; validates technical approach
- **With UX Designers**: Ensures user experience aligns with business processes and goals
- **With Project Managers**: Provides input on scope and estimates; tracks requirements changes
- **With Customer Success Managers**: Gathers customer feedback to inform requirements; validates solutions meet customer needs

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered designs that are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with business goals. They conduct user research, create prototypes, and validate designs through usability testing.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability studies
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design user flows and information architecture
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design standards
- Collaborate with developers on design implementation
- Validate designs through user testing and feedback

### Goals
- Deliver delightful, intuitive user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support costs
- Ensure consistency across product experiences
- Advocate for user needs in product decisions

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and persona presentations
- Prototype walkthroughs with stakeholders
- Design handoff documentation for developers

### Inter-role Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Aligns design with product strategy; conducts user research to inform roadmap
- **With Business Analysts**: Ensures designs support business processes and requirements
- **With Developers**: Provides design specifications and assets; reviews implementation for design fidelity
- **With Customer Success Managers**: Gathers user feedback; identifies usability pain points
- **With QA/Testing**: Collaborates on usability testing; defines acceptance criteria for UI/UX

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads ensure security is built into products and processes. They identify vulnerabilities, define security requirements, oversee security testing, and guide teams on secure coding practices.

### Responsibilities
- Define security requirements and acceptance criteria
- Conduct threat modeling and security reviews
- Oversee security testing (SAST, DAST, penetration testing)
- Review code for security vulnerabilities
- Maintain security incident response procedures
- Ensure compliance with security policies and regulations

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities in production
- Minimize security incident impact and recovery time
- Build security awareness across the team
- Maintain compliance with security standards

### Typical Communication
- Security design reviews and threat modeling sessions
- Vulnerability assessments and remediation planning
- Security training and awareness communications
- Incident response coordination and post-mortems

### Inter-role Interactions
- **With Developers**: Provides secure coding guidance; reviews code for security issues; advises on security libraries
- **With Project Managers**: Escalates security risks; advises on security testing timelines
- **With QA/Testing**: Defines security test cases; reviews test coverage for security scenarios
- **With Product Managers**: Advises on security feature requirements; balances security with usability
- **With Business Analysts**: Ensures security requirements are captured in specifications

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers ensure customers achieve their desired outcomes using the product. They gather customer feedback, identify adoption barriers, advocate for customer needs, and drive product improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Build relationships with key customers and understand their goals
- Monitor customer health metrics and usage patterns
- Gather and prioritize customer feedback and feature requests
- Coordinate customer onboarding and training
- Escalate customer issues and advocate for resolutions
- Provide customer insights to inform product roadmap

### Goals
- Increase customer satisfaction and retention
- Drive product adoption and usage
- Reduce churn through proactive engagement
- Inform product strategy with customer insights

### Typical Communication
- Regular customer check-ins and business reviews
- Customer feedback summaries for product teams
- Escalation communications for critical customer issues
- Success stories and case studies

### Inter-role Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Provides customer feedback and feature requests; validates product roadmap against customer needs
- **With Business Analysts**: Shares customer requirements and use cases; validates solutions meet customer needs
- **With UX Designers**: Provides user feedback and usability issues; facilitates customer research sessions
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinates customer communications on releases; manages expectations on timelines
- **With Developers**: Coordinates customer escalations; provides context on critical issues

## Cross-Role Collaboration Patterns

### Project Kickoff
- **Project Manager** facilitates kickoff meeting
- **Product Manager** presents vision and success criteria
- **Business Analyst** shares requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Scrum Master** establishes team working agreements and ceremonies
- **UX Designer** presents user research and design direction
- **Security Lead** conducts initial threat assessment
- **Developers** provide technical feasibility input

### Sprint Planning
- **Scrum Master** facilitates planning session
- **Product Manager** clarifies priorities and business value
- **Business Analyst** answers requirements questions
- **Developers** estimate and commit to work
- **UX Designer** provides design assets and clarifications
- **Security Lead** identifies security stories and requirements

### Release Preparation
- **Project Manager** coordinates release timeline and communications
- **Developers** complete implementation and code review
- **QA/Testing** validates acceptance criteria and runs test suites
- **Security Lead** approves security testing and vulnerability remediation
- **UX Designer** validates design implementation
- **Customer Success Manager** prepares customer communications and training

### Issue Escalation
- **Tier 1**: Team discusses in standup; Scrum Master removes impediments
- **Tier 2**: Project Manager escalates to stakeholders; Security Lead involved for security issues
- **Tier 3**: Product Manager and sponsor make priority/scope decisions
- **Customer Impact**: Customer Success Manager coordinates customer communications
